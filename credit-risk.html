Credit Risk Analytics

An end-to-end credit risk analytics project analysing customer behaviour, affordability indicators, and policy trade-offs using SQL, Python, and Power BI.

GitHub Repo View Report (PDF)

Project Overview

This project analyses consumer credit risk at a portfolio level, with the goal of identifying high-risk customer segments and evaluating how credit policy decisions impact approval volumes and default rates.

The workflow follows a SQL-first approach for data preparation and feature engineering, followed by exploratory analysis and interactive dashboard development in Power BI.

Analytical Approach

  • SQL joins and aggregations to construct a customer-level analytical dataset
  • Feature engineering of affordability and behavioural risk indicators
  • Exploratory analysis to validate risk patterns and segment behaviour
  • Policy-driven scenario analysis using credit-to-income (CTI) thresholds

Dashboard Highlights

Executive Risk Overview

Portfolio-level view of default rates, risk concentration, and key affordability metrics.

Customer Risk Segmentation

Segment-level analysis highlighting elevated default risk by age, income, and prior refusals.

Policy & Scenario Analysis

Evaluation of approval–risk trade-offs under varying credit-to-income (CTI) thresholds.

Key Insights

  • Aggregate portfolio metrics conceal concentrated risk within specific customer segments
  • Younger customers in lower income bands exhibit disproportionately higher default risk
  • Previous credit refusals are a strong behavioural predictor of future default
  • Relaxing CTI thresholds increases approvals but accelerates default risk beyond optimal levels
