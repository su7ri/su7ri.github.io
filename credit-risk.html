Data Analytics Project

Credit Risk Analytics
& Policy Scenario Analysis

An end-to-end credit risk analytics project analysing customer behaviour, affordability indicators, and policy trade-offs using SQL, Python, and Power BI.

Project Overview

This project focuses on analysing consumer credit risk at a portfolio level, with the aim of identifying high-risk customer segments and evaluating the impact of credit policy decisions on approval volumes and default rates.

The analysis follows an end-to-end workflow, starting with SQL-based data preparation and feature engineering, followed by exploratory analysis and interactive dashboard development in Power BI.

Analytical Approach

  • SQL joins and aggregations to prepare an analysis-ready dataset
  • Exploratory analysis to validate patterns and identify risk drivers
  • Power BI dashboards to communicate insights and support decision-making
  • Scenario analysis using credit-to-income (CTI) thresholds

Dashboard Highlights

Executive Risk Overview

Provides a portfolio-level summary of default rates, high-risk customer distribution, and key affordability indicators.

Customer Risk Segmentation

Analyses default behaviour across demographic and behavioural segments, highlighting concentrated risk pockets by age, income, and previous credit refusals.

Policy & Scenario Analysis

Evaluates trade-offs between approval volumes and default risk by simulating different credit-to-income (CTI) thresholds.

Key Insights

  • Portfolio-level default rates mask concentrated risk within specific customer segments
  • Younger customers in lower income bands exhibit disproportionately higher default risk
  • Previous credit refusals are a strong behavioural predictor of future default
  • Relaxing CTI thresholds increases approvals but accelerates default risk beyond optimal levels
