Project Overview

This project analyses UK National Rail journey data to evaluate operational reliability, ticket pricing dynamics, customer purchasing behaviour, and route-level performance.

A SQL-first workflow was used for data preparation and KPI development, followed by Python-based cleaning and Power BI dashboarding for insight delivery.

Analytical Approach

  • SQL joins and aggregations to construct journey-level and route-level datasets
  • Feature engineering for delays, cancellations, pricing bands, and peak indicators
  • Exploratory analysis to identify operational and commercial patterns
  • Power BI dashboards to communicate KPIs to non-technical stakeholders

Key Insights & Decisions

  • Morning peak services exhibit the highest delay rates → targeted operational focus required
  • Weekend services underperform due to maintenance schedules → opportunity for timetable optimisation
  • First Class pricing is disproportionately higher than Standard → revenue sensitivity risk
  • Online customers favour lower-priced tickets → digital pricing strategies can be optimised
  • A small number of routes account for the majority of delays and revenue → prioritisation opportunity

SQL KPI Example


SELECT
  route,
  COUNT(*) AS journeys,
  AVG(delay_minutes) AS avg_delay,
  ROUND(
    SUM(CASE WHEN delay_minutes <= 5 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)::DECIMAL
    / COUNT(*) * 100, 2
  ) AS on_time_pct
FROM railway_journeys
GROUP BY route
ORDER BY avg_delay DESC;

This query underpins route-level performance analysis and prioritisation of operational improvements.
