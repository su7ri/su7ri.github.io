UK Railway Analytics

A complete end-to-end data analytics project exploring operational performance, pricing trends, customer behaviour, and route performance across 31,000+ rail journeys in the UK.

GitHub Repo View Report (PDF)

Project Summary

This project analyses National Rail journey data from January to April 2024. The goal was to understand on-time performance, ticket pricing behaviour, customer purchase trends, and route performance. The final output includes a multi-page Power BI dashboard, a detailed PDF report, and a set of business insights designed for operational and commercial teams.

Key Metrics

Total Journeys: 31,653
On-time Performance: 86.82%
Delayed: 7.24%
Cancelled: 5.94%
Average Ticket Price: ~£36
Online Purchase Rate: 58.51%

Insights

1. Operational Performance

  • Most services run on time, but morning peak (09:00–09:30) suffers the highest delays.
  • Weekend journeys show weaker performance due to maintenance patterns.
  • Stable operations strongly correlate with higher revenue periods.

2. Pricing Analysis

  • First Class tickets cost more than twice Standard Class on average.
  • Most ticket purchases fall below £60.
  • Railcard users benefit from significantly lower fares.

3. Customer Behaviour

  • Online purchases exceed station purchases (~58%).
  • Clear patterns across peak/off-peak buying behaviour.
  • Digital buyers tend to purchase cheaper ticket types.

4. Route Performance

  • A few major routes carry most passenger volume.
  • Some corridors experience extremely high delay rates.
  • Revenue is concentrated in long-distance intercity routes.
